## 1. Purpose
The parties wish to exchange confidential information to evaluate and/or carry out a **performance-marketing engagement or white-label partnership** (the "Purpose").

## 2. Confidential Information
"**Confidential Information**" means any non-public information disclosed by one party ("Discloser") to the other ("Recipient"), in any form, including: business and pricing data, strategies, account and campaign data, client/customer identities and lists, audits, analyses, reports, know-how, and the existence and terms of any partnership. It includes information that is marked confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

## 3. Obligations
The Recipient shall:
- use Confidential Information **solely for the Purpose**;
- **not disclose** it to third parties without the Discloser's prior written consent (save to employees/sub-processors with a need to know who are bound by equivalent confidentiality);
- protect it with at least reasonable care; and
- **not use** it to solicit or compete for the Discloser's clients (this is in addition to any separate Non-Solicitation Agreement).

## 4. Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes public through no fault of the Recipient; (b) was lawfully known to the Recipient before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of the Confidential Information; or (d) is lawfully received from a third party without restriction. Disclosure required by law/authority is permitted with prior notice to the Discloser where lawful.

## 5. Term & return
This NDA applies for the engagement/evaluation and confidentiality obligations survive for **`[3]` years** after disclosure. On request or on termination, the Recipient returns or destroys Confidential Information (subject to legal retention duties).

## 6. No license; no obligation
No intellectual-property licence is granted. This NDA does not oblige either party to enter into any further agreement.

## 7. Final provisions
- Governed by the **laws of the Federal Republic of Germany**, excluding the UN CISG.
- Place of jurisdiction: `[German court venue, e.g. Berlin]`, to the extent validly agreeable between businesses.
- Text-form changes only; severability applies. The Discloser is entitled to **injunctive relief** for threatened breaches.
