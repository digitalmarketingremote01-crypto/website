## 1. Purpose & status
The Provider delivers performance-marketing fulfilment **on a white-label basis** for the Partner's own clients ("**End Clients**"). Both parties act exclusively as entrepreneurs (Unternehmer, § 14 BGB); this is a B2B agreement. The Provider acts as an **independent contractor** — this Agreement creates no employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency-by-representation relationship.

## 2. White-label services
On the Partner's instruction, the Provider delivers (per Annex A): Google Ads management, Meta Ads management, conversion tracking & analytics, and reporting — **invisibly in the background, under the Partner's brand.** The End Client sees only the Partner's brand.

## 3. Invisibility & no direct contact
The Provider works **behind the scenes** and will **not identify itself to the Partner's End Clients** as the performing party. The Provider will **not contact, solicit, or communicate directly with End Clients** except where the Partner expressly requests it in writing (e.g. to join a call). The **client relationship belongs to the Partner.** Client protection is governed by the separate **Non-Solicitation / Client-Protection Agreement (Document 03)**, which forms an integral part of this Agreement.

## 4. White-label reporting
Reports are delivered **neutral and unbranded — without any "Digital Marketing Remote" or Provider name or mark** — so the Partner can present them as its own work. On request, the Provider provides reports in the Partner's template/branding using materials the Partner supplies.

## 5. Sales support (optional)
On request, the Provider supports the Partner's new-business efforts with account audits, analyses and pitch material to help the Partner win End Clients. This support is likewise white-label and does not create direct Provider–End-Client contact.

## 6. Accounts, data & ownership
All advertising/tracking accounts are owned by the **End Client or the Partner**. The Provider works **on access rights only** and never takes ownership. Advertising budget is funded by the End Client/Partner directly to the platforms; the Provider does not fund or margin media spend.

## 7. Fees, term & termination
- **Partner fee:** the per-service wholesale fee in Annex A, **individually agreed per partner** based on the scope discussed (it may differ from any list prices). The **Partner sets its own price/markup to its End Clients at its sole discretion**; the Partner's margin is its own.
- **Billing:** monthly **in advance, payable before the service month begins**; fees are **net** and **non-refundable** once a service month has started.
- **Late payment / suspension:** the Provider may **pause or suspend** fulfilment if payment is overdue, and charge **default interest (§ 288 BGB)**.
- **Fee changes:** the Provider may adjust fees for **future** months with `[30]` days' notice; the Partner may terminate before the change applies. Scope changes are quoted separately.
- **Term:** **flexible, monthly, with no rigid minimum term.** Either party may terminate with **`[14]` days' notice to month-end** in text form; per-End-Client engagements wind down on `[30]` days' notice for a clean handover. The **Provider may terminate with immediate effect** for non-payment or material breach.
- Taxes: net fees, **reverse charge (§ 13b UStG)** — the Partner accounts for VAT in its country.

## 8. Confidentiality
Each party keeps the other's confidential information (including End Client identities, account data, strategies, pricing) secret, using it only to perform this Agreement. Survives termination for `[3]` years. A separate Mutual NDA (Document 05) may apply in addition.

## 9. Data protection
For any processing of personal data, the parties conclude the **Data Processing Agreement / AVV (Art. 28 GDPR — Document 04)**. Where the Partner acts as processor for its End Client and engages the Provider as **sub-processor**, the AVV is concluded on that basis. The AVV prevails for all data-protection matters.

## 10. No guarantee of results; liability; indemnity
Beyond any performance guarantee expressly agreed in Annex A, the Provider warrants a **diligent professional service, not specific advertising results** (see reasoning in Document 01, Clause 7). Liability is limited as in Document 01, Clause 12 (no liability for slight negligence except essential-obligation breaches, capped at fees paid in the preceding `[3]` months; no indirect/consequential damage, lost profit, media spend, or platform decisions). The Provider is **not liable for events beyond its reasonable control** (force majeure, platform outages or account actions).

The **Partner is solely responsible** for its End-Client relationships and for the End Clients' products, offers, and the content, claims and legality of advertisements, and **indemnifies and holds the Provider harmless** against third-party claims, fines, platform actions and costs arising from End-Client content, the Partner's instructions, or any breach of platform policy or law. The Provider may **decline or stop** work concerning prohibited, illegal or high-risk activities, or that would breach platform policy.
